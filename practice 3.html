Spaghetti Aglio e Olio



What is Aglio e Olio?

Aglio e olio is a traditional Italian pasta dish from Naples. It is a typical dish of Neapolitan cuisine. The dish is made by lightly sautéing sliced, minced, or pressed garlic in olive oil, sometimes with the addition of dried red chili flakes, and tossing with spaghetti.


INGREDIENTS

    400g (14 oz) spaghetti
     4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
     1 glass of extra virgin olive oil (about 240 ml) (1 cup)
     1 peperoncino, seeded and chopped
     fresh parsley, chopped (optional)
     salt, to taste

    INSTRUCTIONS

    1. Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil.

    2. Then, add spaghetti and while they're cooking, heat the olive oil in a saucepan and start gently sautéing garlic and peperoncino over low heat.

    3. Add a splash of pasta water to the pan to stop the cooking, then transfer the al dente spaghetti using tongs to the pan.

    4. Toss to combine.

    5. Lastly, if desired, add chopped parsley and toss again. Serve immediately.

    How to Store Leftovers

    This dish is best served fresh, as the oil tends to separate from the pasta after being refrigerated. It is still just as delicious, however you may need to take an extra step in re-heating if the oil bothers you. Your leftovers will last 3-5 days in the fridge. To reheat, you can simply microwave it. To reduce the amount of separated oil in your bowl, reheat your leftovers in a skillet over medium-heat with a splash of water. Continually toss the pasta well as it heats up to re-create the sauce.
